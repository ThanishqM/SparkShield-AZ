# 🚀 Future Roadmap

This project is an early prototype focused on risk awareness. If developed further, we see several meaningful directions to expand its impact.

---

## 🏥 Integration with Healthcare Systems

One of the most important next steps is connecting the system with local health centers.

* If a user’s symptoms and risk level cross a certain threshold, the system could:

  * Flag the case for further review
  * Share anonymized or user-approved data with healthcare providers
  * Suggest nearby clinics or testing centers

The goal is not just to assess risk, but to **help users take the next step toward diagnosis and care**.

---

## 🧬 Symptom-to-Disease Mapping

Currently, the system focuses on general risk levels.

In the future, we want to:

* Compare reported symptoms with known disease patterns
* Identify possible matches with existing illnesses
* Provide users with more specific guidance

This would move the system closer to **early-stage decision support**, while still avoiding full medical diagnosis.

---

## 🌐 Real-Time Data Integration

We plan to connect with live data sources such as:

* EpiCore API
* Weather APIs
* Public health dashboards

This would make risk scores more accurate and responsive to real-world changes.

---

## 📱 Mobile Accessibility

To increase adoption:

* Develop a mobile-friendly version or app
* Enable quick daily symptom check-ins
* Add push notifications for alerts

---

## 🗺️ Community Risk Visualization

* Map risk levels across regions in Arizona
* Identify emerging hotspots
* Help public health officials monitor trends

---

## 🤖 Improved AI Models

* Transition from rule-based scoring to trained ML models
* Incorporate historical outbreak data
* Improve personalization of risk assessments

---

## 🔐 Privacy and Data Controls

As the system scales:

* Add stronger user consent mechanisms
* Allow users to control what data is shared
* Ensure compliance with public health data standards

---

## 🧠 Long-Term Vision

This system is not just about predicting risk.

It aims to become a **bridge between individuals and public health systems**, helping:

* detect early signals
* guide user decisions
* support faster response to outbreaks

---

## 🏁 Summary

Future development focuses on:

* moving from awareness → action
* connecting users → healthcare systems
* improving accuracy with real data

Ultimately, the goal is to make early outbreak detection **more accessible, actionable, and community-driven**.
